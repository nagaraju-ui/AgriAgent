# Cotton
Cotton is a kharif crop suited to black and well-drained soils. It needs warm weather
and moderate, well-distributed rainfall. Sowing typically begins with the onset of the
monsoon. Excess late rain at boll opening can reduce quality.

# Paddy
Paddy needs assured water and is suited to clayey soils that retain moisture. It is a
water-intensive kharif crop; transplanting is common where irrigation is available.

# Groundnut
Groundnut is drought-tolerant and grows well in light, sandy and red soils. It suits
areas with limited rainfall and is a common choice in dry zones such as Anantapur.

# Maize
Maize grows in a wide range of soils but needs more water than groundnut. It responds
well to good rainfall and fertiliser, giving higher yield but higher input cost.

# Chilli
Chilli is a high-value crop grown widely around Guntur. It needs warm weather and
assured irrigation; heat and water stress at flowering reduce yield.

# Tomato
Tomato is sensitive to oversupply gluts that crash prices at harvest. Staggered
planting and cold storage access reduce market risk.
